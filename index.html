About me

I am Dr. Hillary Kipruto, a public health specialist currently working on strengthening health information systems, digital health, and knowledge management across African countries, and I am currently a Public Health doctoral student at the University of Pretoria. My work focuses on strengthening national health information systems, civil registration and vital statistics, digital health, and the Regional Data Hub.

I support countries to improve data generation, analysis, dissemination, and use for decision-making, and I often work at the intersection of policy, data science, and capacity building. I am also pursuing a PhD in Public Health (Health Systems/Global Health) with research interests in service interruptions, reporting burden, and digital transformation in sub-national health systems.

My interest in generative AI is driven by a simple question: how do we turn messy, multi-source health data into clean, human-readable, decision-ready intelligence – faster?

About the course

"Generative AI for Work & Research" is a practice-oriented course designed to help professionals, researchers, and technical officers apply AI tools to real-world workflows. The course pillars I am focusing on are:

1. Prompting for policy & health briefs

Drafting concept notes, memos, meeting talking points, and country feedback notes faster – with AI as a co-writer.

2. AI for data & DHIS2 narratives

Turning HIS, RDHUB or DHIS2 data exports into analytical narratives, dashboards, and PowerPoint-ready insights.

3. Research acceleration

Automating literature scans, mapping to WHO frameworks, generating data collection tools, and structuring mixed-methods write-ups.

4. Safe & ethical AI

Ensuring data security, protecting personal information, and keeping country materials confidential.

This portfolio will evolve as I add: policy drafts generated with AI; clean indicator metadata tables; AI-assisted slide content for WHO-FIC, SCORE and HHFA; and prototypes for conversational data access in the WHO AFRO Regional Data Hub.
